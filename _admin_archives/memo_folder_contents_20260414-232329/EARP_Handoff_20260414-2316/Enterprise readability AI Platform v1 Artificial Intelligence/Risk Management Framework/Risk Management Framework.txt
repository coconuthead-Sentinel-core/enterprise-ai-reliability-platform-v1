Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework
Implementation Packet (NIST AI RMF 1.0 Aligned)
Version: 2.0
LLM Scope Statement
This artifact applies to large language model (LLM) product engineering, evaluation, release governance, and operational reliability in 2026 enterprise environments.


Document Control
- Purpose: operationalize AI risk management for enterprise software contexts
- Source Standard: NIST AI RMF 1.0
- Review Cadence: quarterly, or on major system change
- Owner: Governance and Risk Management Office

1. Framework Purpose
Establish measurable controls for AI system safety, reliability, fairness, accountability, and regulatory readiness.

2. Scope
Applicable to design, deployment, monitoring, and retirement phases for AI-enabled software systems.

3. GOVERN Function (Organizational Governance)
- Define risk ownership and accountability matrix.
- Establish AI policy set, control objectives, and approval workflows.
- Require documented evidence for release-governance decisions.

4. MAP Function (Context and Risk Framing)
- Define system context, intended users, and prohibited use.
- Identify stakeholders and potential impact populations.
- Document risk scenarios, assumptions, and dependencies.

5. MEASURE Function (Assessment and Validation)
- Define reliability, robustness, and monitoring metrics.
- Execute test protocols and bias/impact checks where applicable.
- Track model and system performance drift over time.

6. MANAGE Function (Response and Improvement)
- Implement mitigation plans for identified risks.
- Define escalation paths and incident response triggers.
- Reassess controls after changes and production incidents.

7. Profile Method
- Current-state profile: document existing control maturity.
- Target-state profile: define expected maturity by milestone.
- Gap closure cycle: prioritize by risk severity and business impact.

8. Legal-Readiness Evidence Checklist
- Governance policy set
- Risk and control register
- Validation and test records
- Incident response procedures
- Change approvals and decision logs
- Ownership and licensing records

9. Release-Readiness Criteria
A system is documentation-ready for legal review when governance, risk, test, and evidence controls are complete and approval records are signed.

10. Notes
This packet is a technical and governance framework for legal readiness preparation. It is not legal advice; final legal conclusions require licensed counsel.
