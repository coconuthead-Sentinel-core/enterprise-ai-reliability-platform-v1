LLM Scope Statement
This document establishes market viability for an LLM reliability governance platform and links technical performance to commercial outcomes.

1. Market Segmentation
- Enterprise software organizations shipping LLM features
- Regulated industries with audit and governance obligations
- Mid-market teams requiring low-overhead release assurance

2. Commercial Model
- B2B subscription by active environment and monthly evaluated release candidates
- Optional premium analytics for model-risk trend intelligence

3. Quantitative Market Framework
- TAM: total addressable spend on AI reliability and governance tooling
- SAM: serviceable segment for LLM release-governance tools
- SOM: target share based on integration and sales capacity

4. Pricing and Revenue Assumptions
- Base subscription: USD 199 to 499 per active team per month
- Enterprise tier: annual contract with governance support
- Revenue equation: MRR = active_accounts * average_monthly_price

5. Primary Use Cases
UC-1 LLM Release Readiness Evaluation
UC-2 LLM Safety and Compliance Evidence Export
UC-3 Incident-to-Decision Causality Analysis
UC-4 Executive Reliability and Unit-Economics Reporting

6. Verifiable Product-Market Evidence
- Pilot conversion rate from trial to paid deployment
- Reduction in failed production releases
- Reduction in compliance remediation cycle time
- Positive ROI based on avoided incident and rollback costs

7. Market-Readiness Criteria
The product is market-ready when reliability thresholds, security controls, and pricing viability are all validated in pilot cohorts.


8. Consumer Market Track
Consumer-facing deployment is supported through a governed feature subset.
- Consumer onboarding success rate target: >= 85%
- 30-day retention target: >= 40%
- Consumer support ticket rate target: <= 0.10 per active user per month
- Consumer safety escalation target: <= 0.5% of sessions

9. Consumer Readiness Evidence
- Usability test completion with statistically significant sample size
- Safety and inclusivity checks pass on consumer prompt sets
- Pricing acceptance validated through willingness-to-pay testing
