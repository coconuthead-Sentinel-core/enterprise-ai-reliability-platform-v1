Business Case
Version: 2.1

LLM Scope Statement
This business case evaluates commercial viability for a large language model reliability governance platform.

1. Problem
Organizations deploying LLM features face high risk from hallucinations, unsafe outputs, inconsistent release controls, and fragmented evidence systems.

2. Solution
EARP unifies LLM evaluation evidence, deterministic release policies, and audit exports in a single operational control plane.

3. Buyer and User Model
Buyer: VP Engineering, Head of Platform, Chief Risk/Compliance Officer.
Primary users: release managers, reliability engineers, AI evaluators, security analysts.

4. Revenue and Pricing Model
- Team subscription: USD 199 to 499 per month
- Enterprise contract: annual pricing with governance support
- Optional premium analytics add-on

5. Unit Economics Equations
- MRR = paying_accounts * average_monthly_price
- ARR = 12 * MRR
- GrossMargin = (revenue - hosting_and_support_cost) / revenue
- CACPaybackMonths = CAC / monthly_gross_profit_per_account

6. Pilot Business Targets
- Trial-to-paid conversion >= 20%
- Monthly logo churn <= 3%
- Time-to-first-value <= 14 days
- Documented customer ROI within one quarter

7. Investment Readiness Evidence
- Measured improvement in release quality metrics
- Measured reduction in incident cost and remediation effort
- Signed enterprise design-partner feedback

8. Legal-Readiness Note
This document supports legal and commercial due diligence. Final legal determinations require licensed counsel.


9. Consumer Commercial Validation
If launched as a consumer tier, the business case requires:
- validated onboarding funnel conversion
- acceptable support cost per active user
- retention stability after month 1
- documented safety and content-policy adherence for public users
